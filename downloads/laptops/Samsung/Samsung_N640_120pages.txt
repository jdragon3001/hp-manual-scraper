FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS:

Q1: How can I resolve the issue if my Samsung N640 laptop won't turn on?
A1: If your Samsung N640 laptop does not turn on, first ensure that the battery is sufficiently charged or connected via the power adapter. Check if the power light is on and that the power cord is securely connected. If these steps do not work, attempt a hard reset by removing the battery and power adapter, then pressing and holding the power button for about 15 seconds before reconnecting everything and trying to power on the device again.

Q2: Some of the letter keys type numbers instead of letters on my Samsung N640. How can I fix this?
A2: This issue may be caused by the Num Lock feature being activated on your Samsung N640 laptop. Check if the Num Lock light on your keyboard is on. If it is, press the 'Num Lock' key to deactivate it. This will return the keypad keys to typing letters.

Q3: What should I do if there's nothing displayed on the LCD panel of my Samsung N640?
A3: If nothing appears on the LCD panel of your Samsung N640, first check the brightness adjustment. If you are using an external monitor, press <Fn+F5> to switch back to the LCD panel.

Q4: How should I proceed if my Samsung N640 won't connect to online services via modem?
A4: Start by checking the quality of your phone line, as interference or poor line power can affect modem connectivity. If issues persist, consider reducing the modem's communication speed. Additionally, ensure that all on-line services are operational and that you have the correct dialing prefix when using a PBX system.

Q5: Why does my Samsung N640 display random characters on the screen during communications?
A5: Random or garbage characters on the screen during modem communication may be due to a mismatch in terminal modes between your communication service and program. Ensure that the terminal modes match to resolve this issue.

Q6: What is HDMI?
A6: HDMI stands for High-Definition Multimedia Interface. An HDMI cable is used to transport audio and video signals between devices.

Q7: What is bluetooth?
A7: Bluetooth is a way of exchanging data wirelessly between electronic devices via radio waves. The distance between the two devices that exchange data can in most cases be no more than ten metres.

Q8: What screen dimensions does my laptop have?
A8: The size of your laptop is usually expressed in inches, 1 inch is 2.54 centimetres. The size is the diagonal size of your laptop, so you measure the screen from bottom left to top right.

Q9: How can I best clean my laptop?
A9: A slightly damp cleaning cloth or soft, dust-free cloth works best to remove fingerprints. Dust in hard-to-reach places is best removed with compressed air.

Q10: What does the download speed on the internet depend on?
A10: The download speed on the internet depends on the type of connection you have. If you have a 25 Mb/s connection this means that theoretically you should be able to download 25 MB per second.

Q11: How do I clean my keyboard?
A11: Hold the keyboard upside down and use a can of compressed air to blow away the dirt. The keys can even be cleaned with a damp cloth.
