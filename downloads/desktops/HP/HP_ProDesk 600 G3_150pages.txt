FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS:

Q1: How can I properly orient the HP ProDesk 600 G3 Small Form Factor computer in a tower orientation?
A1: To properly orient the HP ProDesk 600 G3 Small Form Factor computer in a tower orientation, follow these steps:
1. Remove any security devices hindering movement.
2. Take out all removable media like CDs or USB drives.
3. Shut down the computer correctly and turn off external devices.
4. Unplug the power cord from the AC outlet and disconnect external devices.
5. Position the computer with its right side facing down and place it in the optional stand.
6. Reconnect the power cord and external devices, then power on the computer.
7. Ensure there is a clearance of at least 10.2 centimeters (4 inches) on all sides of the computer.
8. Lock any security devices that were disengaged before moving the computer.

Q2: What precautions should I take before moving the HP ProDesk 600 G3 Small Form Factor computer to a tower orientation?
A2: 1. Remove any security devices hindering movement.
2. Take out all removable media like CDs or USB drives.
3. Shut down the computer correctly and turn off external devices.
4. Unplug the power cord and disconnect external devices to avoid voltage damage.
5. Position the computer with the right side down and place it in the optional stand.
6. Reconnect the power cord and external devices, then power on the computer.
7. Ensure there is a clearance of at least 10.2 centimeters (4 inches) around the computer.
8. Lock any security devices previously disengaged.

Q3: How do I remove and replace components in the Small Form Factor computer when using it in a tower orientation?
A3: To remove and replace components in the HP ProDesk 600 G3 Small Form Factor computer when using it in a tower orientation, follow these steps:
1. Remove any security devices hindering movement.
2. Take out all removable media like CDs or USB drives.
3. Shut down the computer correctly and power off external devices.
4. Unplug the power cord from the AC outlet and disconnect external devices to prevent damage.
5. Position the computer with the right side facing down and place it in the optional stand.
6. Reconnect the power cord and external devices, then power on the computer, ensuring 10.2 centimeters (4 inches) of clearance on all sides.
7. Lock any security devices previously disengaged.
For specific component replacement like the system board:
1. Prepare for disassembly.
2. Remove the access panel.
3. Take out the drive cage.
4. When replacing the system board, transfer memory modules, expansion cards, fan sink, and processor.
5. Disconnect all data and power cables.
6. Remove the front panel by rotating the left side upward and lifting it off the front of the computer.

Q4: What is HDMI?
A4: HDMI stands for High-Definition Multimedia Interface. An HDMI cable is used to transport audio and video signals between devices.

Q5: What does the download speed on the internet depend on?
A5: The download speed on the internet depends on the type of connection you have. If you have a 25 Mb/s connection this means that theoretically you should be able to download 25 MB per second.

Q6: How do I clean my keyboard?
A6: Hold the keyboard upside down and use a can of compressed air to blow away the dirt. The keys can even be cleaned with a damp cloth.
