FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS:

Q1: Why is my desktop not turning on?
A1: First, ensure that the power cable is securely plugged into both the wall outlet and the back of the computer. Next, check if the power switch located on the front panel is in the 'on' position. If the issue persists, verify that the power supply unit is functioning correctly and that there are no loose connections inside the computer.

Q2: How can I connect additional displays to my HP Z840?
A2: To connect additional displays, locate the graphics card on the back panel of your computer. Identify the available display output ports (e.g., DisplayPort, HDMI, DVI) and connect the desired cable from the display to the corresponding port on the graphics card. Ensure that the graphics card and the display settings are properly configured in the operating system's display settings.

Q3: How can I upgrade the RAM in my HP Z840?
A3: Firstly, power off your computer and unplug it from the power source. Open the side panel of your computer case and locate the DIMM slots on the motherboard. To upgrade RAM, insert the new DIMM modules into the available slots, ensuring they are properly aligned and securely seated. Close the side panel and power on the computer, which should recognize the increased RAM capacity.

Q4: How do I replace the hard drive in my HP Z840?
A4: Begin by shutting down the computer and disconnecting the power cable. Open the side panel of your computer case and locate the existing hard drive bay(s). Disconnect the cables and screws securing the current hard drive, then remove it from the bay. Install the new hard drive by connecting the necessary cables and securely fastening it in the bay.
