FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS:

Q1: What should I do if my HP Pro 3335 is experiencing power issues?
A1: If your HP Pro 3335 is experiencing power issues, first ensure that it is properly connected to a working power outlet and that the power cable is securely connected. Check the power supply's voltage switch if applicable. If the computer still won't power on, examine the power LED for any blinking lights, which could be an error code indicating the problem source.

Q2: How can I resolve memory problems on my HP Pro 3335?
A2: If you experience memory issues on your HP Pro 3335, reseat the memory modules to ensure they are correctly installed. If problems persist, replace one DIMM at a time to identify a faulty module. It's recommended to use HP-certified memory for compatibility and stability.

Q3: What should I do if my HP Pro 3335's screen is blank and the power LED is flashing red?
A3: If your HP Pro 3335's screen is blank with a flashing red power LED, this could indicate a memory or graphics error. Reseat the DIMMs or graphics card if applicable. If the issue persists, components like the system board or the graphics card may need to be replaced.

Q4: How can I fix network problems with the HP Pro 3335?
A4: To resolve network issues with your HP Pro 3335, ensure all network cables are properly connected. Restart the router and modem and check that network drivers are up-to-date. You may also want to use the HP Network Check utility to diagnose and fix network problems.

Q5: Why won't my HP Pro 3335 boot from CD-ROM or DVD drive?
A5: If your HP Pro 3335 won't boot from the CD-ROM or DVD drive, check that the device is enabled in the BIOS setup under the boot order and storage options. Ensure no non-bootable media is in the drive and that all cable connections are secure.

Q6: What is HDMI?
A6: HDMI stands for High-Definition Multimedia Interface. An HDMI cable is used to transport audio and video signals between devices.

Q7: What does the download speed on the internet depend on?
A7: The download speed on the internet depends on the type of connection you have. If you have a 25 Mb/s connection this means that theoretically you should be able to download 25 MB per second.

Q8: How do I clean my keyboard?
A8: Hold the keyboard upside down and use a can of compressed air to blow away the dirt. The keys can even be cleaned with a damp cloth.

Q9: Is the manual of the HP Pro 3335 available in English?
A9: Yes, the manual of the HP Pro 3335 is available in English .
