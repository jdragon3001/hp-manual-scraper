FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS:

Q1: How do I connect my desktop to Wi-Fi?
A1: Click on the Wi-Fi icon located in the bottom right corner of the desktop screen. Select the Wi-Fi network you want to connect to and enter the password when prompted.

Q2: How do I play a DVD on my desktop?
A2: Insert the DVD into the DVD drive. Open a media player such as VLC or Windows Media Player. Click on the "File" or "Open" option, and select the DVD drive from the list. The DVD will start playing automatically.

Q3: Why is my desktop not turning on?
A3: Ensure that the power cable is securely plugged into both the power outlet and the back of your desktop. Check if the power button on the front of the desktop is pressed firmly to turn it on.

Q4: What should I do if my desktop is running slowly?
A4: Close any unnecessary programs or browser tabs that are running concurrently. Additionally, you can perform a disk cleanup to remove temporary files. Finally, consider upgrading your RAM if your desktop has low memory.

Q5: My desktop is making loud noises, what should I do?
A5: Make sure that the desktop is placed on a stable and level surface. If the noise persists, it may indicate a problem with the cooling system or a loose component. It is advisable to contact technical support to have it inspected and repaired if necessary.

Q6: What is HDMI?
A6: HDMI stands for High-Definition Multimedia Interface. An HDMI cable is used to transport audio and video signals between devices.

Q7: What does the download speed on the internet depend on?
A7: The download speed on the internet depends on the type of connection you have. If you have a 25 Mb/s connection this means that theoretically you should be able to download 25 MB per second.

Q8: How do I clean my keyboard?
A8: Hold the keyboard upside down and use a can of compressed air to blow away the dirt. The keys can even be cleaned with a damp cloth.

Q9: Is the manual of the Packard Bell iMedia available in English?
A9: Yes, the manual of the Packard Bell iMedia is available in English .
